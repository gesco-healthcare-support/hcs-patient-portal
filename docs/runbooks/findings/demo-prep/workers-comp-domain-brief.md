# California Workers' Compensation Domain Briefing

**Heads-up corrections vs current app text:**

- Dashboard subtitle reads `CCR Sec. 31.5 / 60 days` -- this is the
  pre-amendment number. **Current bar is 90 days (waivable to 120)**
  under the recent CCR 31.5 amendments. An informed audience member
  will flag this. Plan to update before demo or acknowledge.
- ML codes: legacy ML-101..ML-106 (incl. ML-104 complex
  comprehensive) -- current fee schedule uses **ML-201 (initial),
  ML-202 (follow-up), ML-203 (supplemental)** with MLPRR per-page
  record-review charge for records over the threshold.
- The actual report-deadline rules live in **8 CCR 38**, not 31.5.
  Section 31.5 is the enforcement hook (grounds for replacement
  panel) that incorporates the Section 38 deadlines.

## 1. QME Process

**QME = Qualified Medical Evaluator** -- a physician DWC-certified to
perform independent medical-legal evaluations. QMEs do NOT treat
patients; they resolve disputes about workplace injury and benefits.

**Panel selection.** DWC Medical Unit (not the parties) randomly
compiles a panel of THREE QMEs in the requested specialty.
Unrepresented: worker files Form 105. Represented (since Oct 2015):
requesting party files Form 106 electronically; panel issues
immediately. Medical Director excludes business partners / shared
financial interests.

**Strike process.** Represented cases (Labor Code 4062.2(c)): each
party strikes one name within **10 days** of panel assignment (plus
5 days for mail). Remaining QME becomes evaluator. Unrepresented:
worker picks one within 10 days.

**Panel QME vs Regular QME.** "Panel QME" = QME chosen from a DWC-
issued panel via the Form 105/106 process. "QME" alone = the broader
credential.

**Panel exhausted.** Under 8 CCR 31.5, replacement panel may be
requested if: remaining QME unavailable, refuses, was a prior
treater, panel >24 months old, or QME fails to schedule within
90/120 days. After *Vazquez v. Inocensio Renteria* (May 2025),
replacement for unavailability is no longer automatic -- WCAB has
discretion to order replacement for good cause.

## 2. AME Process

**AME = Agreed Medical Evaluator** -- a physician selected by
**mutual agreement** between the represented worker's attorney and
the claims administrator, bypassing the panel process. Only
**represented** workers can use an AME.

**When applies.** Labor Code 4062.2: when there is a 4060/4061/4062
dispute and the worker is represented, parties must FIRST attempt to
agree on an AME before requesting a QME panel.

**Why choose AME over QME.** AME doctor is typically respected by
both sides -> stronger weight at trial, faster settlements, no
strike uncertainty, no replacement-panel disputes. Downside: parties
must agree.

## 3. Roles

- **Injured Worker / Applicant / Patient** -- the employee with the
  injury.
- **Applicant Attorney** -- represents the injured worker (~15%
  contingency).
- **Defense Attorney** -- represents the employer / insurer / TPA.
- **Claim Examiner** (formerly "Adjuster") -- day-to-day claim
  handler at the insurer / TPA / self-insured employer. "Claims
  Administrator" used interchangeably.
- **Treating Physician** -- ongoing care; files PR-2 progress
  reports.
- **QME / AME** -- independent med-legal evaluator. Does NOT treat.
- **WCAB (Workers' Compensation Appeals Board)** -- adjudicating
  body. Seven-member appellate Board in SF + 23 district offices.

## 4. Key Documents

**Treating physician reports.**
- **PR-2** = periodic progress report (every ~45 days or on
  significant change).
- **PR-3 / PR-4** = Permanent and Stationary reports declaring
  Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI).

**Med-legal reports.**
- Current fee schedule: **ML-201 (initial), ML-202 (follow-up),
  ML-203 (supplemental)** + per-page MLPRR charge for records over
  the threshold.
- Legacy fee schedule: ML-101..ML-106 (ML-104 was complex
  comprehensive, requiring three complexity factors).
- All records served on evaluator must include a **Labor Code
  4062.3 declaration** under penalty of perjury.

**"Joint Declaration".** No single canonical form. Most commonly
refers to the **Labor Code 4062.3 declaration** that accompanies
records served on the evaluator, or to a stipulated joint statement
of issues filed together when parties agree to an AME. If the
booking form uses that exact label, confirm against the legacy app
docs.

## 5. Legal Deadlines

**8 CCR 38** is where the deadlines live (31.5 is the enforcement
hook).

- **Initial / follow-up med-legal report: 30 days** from evaluation
  date.
- **Supplemental report: 60 days** from written request.
- **Scheduling: 90 days** (extended from 60) from appointment
  request, extendable to 120 days by waiver. Failure triggers
  replacement under 31.5(a)(2).

**Labor Code 4060 / 4061 / 4062 -- triggers.**
- 4060: claim **denied or delayed** (compensability disputed).
- 4061: claim accepted, **permanent disability** extent disputed.
- 4062: any other med determination by treating physician
  (temporary disability, P&S, work restrictions, return-to-work,
  apportionment, additional body parts, causation). Treatment-
  necessity disputes go to UR / IMR, not 4062.

**Objection deadlines.** 20 days for represented workers, 30 days
for unrepresented. Panel request may be filed no earlier than 10
days (+5 for mail = 15 days; 20 days out-of-state) after objection.

## 6. Appointment Types

- **QME (Panel QME)** -- in-person comprehensive med-legal exam.
  Output: ML-201 / ML-202 report. Duration: 1-2 hours plus record
  review (ML-104-style complex evals historically averaged 3.5+
  hours).
- **AME** -- same exam mechanics, but evaluator agreed-on. Same
  report output and fee schedule.
- **Deposition** -- in-person sworn questioning of the QME/AME with
  court reporter. Either party schedules. Output: transcript.
  Duration: 1-3 hours.
- **Record Review** -- evaluator's review of records served >=20
  days before exam. Built into comprehensive eval; pages over
  threshold = MLPRR at ~$3/page.
- **Supplemental Medical Report** -- written-only follow-up by the
  same evaluator. No additional in-person exam unless evaluator
  deems one necessary. 60 days from request.
- **Cumulative Trauma evaluation** -- substantively a QME or AME
  exam, but captures a date range (start/end of exposure) because LC
  5412 sets the legal "date of injury" via knowledge / disability
  conjunction (see Section 7).

## 7. Cumulative Trauma vs Specific Injury

**Specific injury**: single identifiable accident on a date. Statute
of limitations runs from incident date.

**Cumulative trauma (CT)**: Labor Code 3208.1 -- repeated minor
traumas over time (carpal tunnel, tendonitis, rotator cuff). No
single accident date.

**LC 5412** sets the legal "date of injury" for CT as the date the
employee both (a) first suffered disability AND (b) knew or should
have known the disability was work-caused.

**Why the booking form has date ranges.** Under LC 5500.5(a),
liability is limited to employers during the **one-year period**
preceding the LC 5412 date of injury or last injurious exposure.
The booking captures the exposure window so the evaluator can
apportion across employers / carriers.

## 8. WCAB Office

WCAB = California's workers' compensation adjudicating body. Two
layers: seven-member Appeals Board (appellate, SF) + 23 district
offices across the state (Bakersfield, Eureka, Redding, Santa Rosa,
Sacramento, SF, Oakland, LA, San Diego, etc.).

**Venue options** (Labor Code 5501.5):
- (a) County of applicant attorney's principal place of business.
- (b) County of injured worker's current residence.
- (c) County of place of industrial injury.

Employer may object within 30 days and move venue to (b) or (c).

**How a worker knows their venue:** DWC ZIP-code locator on
dir.ca.gov; I&A unit at 1-800-736-7401.

## 9. IMR vs Med-Legal Evaluation

**Different processes, different disputes.**

**IMR (Independent Medical Review)** -- since July 1, 2013.
Resolves **medical treatment necessity** disputes. Pipeline:
treating physician requests treatment -> Utilization Review (UR)
decides necessity -> if UR denies, worker requests IMR ->
**anonymous** board-certified physician via Maximus reviews records
(NO in-person exam) -> decision in 30 days (3 days expedited).
**Binding** with narrow appeal grounds (5 enumerated, clear and
convincing evidence, 30-day window to WCAB). Cost: $375/IMR. ~91%
of IMR decisions uphold the UR denial.

**Med-Legal (QME/AME)** -- resolves disputes about the **injury
itself**: compensability (4060), permanent disability (4061), other
med determinations (4062). In-person exam, physician known to
parties, report is evidence at WCAB, not binding final decision.

**Critical overlap.** IMR applies even if an AME recommends the
treatment. The AME's treatment recommendation can still be denied
through UR/IMR.

## 10. Why digitize this

Pain points:
- Strict timelines (30/60/90 days), missing any triggers
  replacement-panel risk.
- Records logistics: 20-day pre-exam service, 4062.3 declaration,
  page-count billing.
- Ex parte rules: all communication in writing, copied to all
  parties; one slip voids the eval.
- Scheduling complexity: max 1 hour waiting-room time, 90-day
  window, multi-location.
- Billing disputes: ML-201/202/203 coding, complexity-factor docs,
  second-review before IBR.
- Report formatting: prescriptive structure (complexity factors,
  history, opinion, declaration); defects = replacement-panel risk.

Many QME clinics outsource scheduling/billing/record-digitization
to QME practice management companies. Purpose-built portal
collapses that outsourced workflow into one system tied to
WCAB/DWC procedural model.
