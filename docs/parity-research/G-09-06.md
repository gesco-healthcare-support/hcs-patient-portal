## 1. ID & Summary

In the OLD app every dashboard counter card was a deep link: clicking a card
navigated to a pre-filtered list. Of OLD's 12 cards, 11 routed through
`counterClicked(...)` and 1 (Pending) used a direct `routerLink`. 8 status
cards led to `/appointment-search?appointmentStatusId=N`; 4 external-role
headcount cards led to `/users?userRoleTypeId=N`.

In NEW, only 3 of the 13 rendered tenant-dashboard cards are clickable
(Pending Requests -> status 1, Approved This Week -> status 2, Rejected This
Week -> status 3), all via `openByStatus(N)` to
`/appointments?appointmentStatus=N`. The other 10 tenant cards have no click
handler. This is a Partial gap: the click-through *mechanism* is correct and
reuses the same status-ID values OLD used (the appointments list page already
consumes `?appointmentStatus=N`), but card coverage is reduced.

The gap is gated on two upstream items, not a standalone defect:
- The 6 stubbed *status* cards (Billed, No-Show, Rescheduled, Cancelled,
  Checked In, Checked Out) can be wired to `openByStatus(N)` immediately --
  their target lists already work -- even while the *counts* stay 0 until the
  day-of-exam / billing states ship.
- The 4 role-headcount click-throughs are gone because the cards themselves
  were dropped (that is G-09-01, not this item). They cannot be restored here.

The CSV phrasing "3 live, 8 stub" conflates two different counts; see
section 4 for the exact reconciliation.

## 4. Expected vs Actual

Count reconciliation (the CSV "3 live, 8 stub" is imprecise; here is the exact
picture from the verified sources):

- Clickable cards: OLD 12 (all), NEW 3 (Pending, Approved, Rejected). The
  "3 live" in the CSV = the 3 cards that are BOTH backed by a live count AND
  clickable.
- Backend live counts: 4 tenant-visible (Pending, Approved, Rejected,
  Approaching Legal Deadline) + 2 host-only (Total Doctors, Total Tenants).
  Note Approaching Legal Deadline is LIVE but NOT clickable -- so "live" and
  "clickable" are not the same set.
- Hard-coded 0 ("stub") cards visible to a tenant user (8): Pending Change
  Requests, Billed This Month, No-Show This Month, Rescheduled This Month,
  Cancelled This Week, Checked In Today, Checked Out Today, plus Total
  Doctors/Total Tenants (tenant-side 0). That is the CSV "8 stub".

Expected (OLD parity intent): every card that maps to a filterable list is a
one-click shortcut into that pre-filtered list.

Actual (NEW): only 3 cards are shortcuts. Of the remaining 10 tenant cards, 6
map cleanly to a status filter the appointments list already supports but are
not wired; 2 are NEW-only; 2 are host concepts.

Net parity delta attributable to THIS item (excluding G-09-01's role cards and
the NEW-only cards): 6 status cards that should be click-through shortcuts are
not. The CSV note correctly scopes the fix as "wire click-through on all real
counters; populate 8 stubbed cards as their states ship" -- i.e. phased.

## 7. Candidate Solutions

### Option A -- Wire click-through on the 6 stubbed status cards now (phased, recommended) [effort: S] [risk: low]

Add `role="button"` + `(click)="openByStatus(N)"` to the 6 status placeholder
cards in `tenant-dashboard.component.html`, using the OLD-faithful status IDs:
Billed -> 11, No-Show -> 4, Rescheduled -> 7 (RescheduledNoBill, per OLD),
Cancelled -> 5 (CancelledNoBill, per OLD), Checked In -> 9, Checked Out -> 10.
Leave the counts at 0 (they populate when the day-of-exam/billing states ship,
which is the upstream work the CSV note references). The target consumer
(`appointment.abstract.component.ts:36-44`) already handles any status ID, so
no backend or list-page change is needed.
- Source: OLD code `dashboard.component.ts:73-107` (the canonical mapping);
  NEW `tenant-dashboard.component.ts:70` (existing helper to reuse).
- Tradeoffs: cards become clickable while still showing 0, so clicking lands
  on an empty filtered list until counts populate. This matches OLD's own
  behavior (a 0-count card in OLD was still clickable). Acceptable.
- Risk: low. Additive template attributes; no logic change.

### Option B -- Defer per-card wiring until each state's count goes live [effort: S, deferred] [risk: low]

Do nothing now; add the `(click)` handler to each card in the same PR that
makes its count live (when Billed/No-Show/Check-in/out states ship). This is
the literal reading of the CSV note ("populate 8 stubbed cards as their states
ship. Phased with those features").
- Source: CSV planning note; matches NEW code comments at
  `tenant-dashboard.component.ts:11-14` ("remaining 8 stay at 0 until day-of-exam
  states ship").
- Tradeoffs: simplest sequencing, no risk of a clickable-but-empty card; but
  leaves the parity gap open longer and risks the wiring being forgotten when
  each feature lands.
- Risk: low; the only downside is schedule, not correctness.

### Option C -- Wire all status cards now AND extend OLD-faithful filters to cover paired NoBill/Late states [effort: M] [risk: medium]

Same as A, but for Cancelled and Rescheduled, navigate with a multi-status
filter (5+6, 7+8) rather than OLD's single NoBill variant, since NEW split each
into NoBill/Late states. Requires the appointments list filter to accept
multiple status IDs (currently a single `appointmentStatus` value).
- Source: NEW enum `AppointmentStatusType.cs:14-17` (paired states); OLD only
  filtered the NoBill variant, so this is an improvement-not-parity choice.
- Tradeoffs: more faithful to user intent ("show me cancelled appointments")
  but diverges from OLD's exact filter AND requires a list-filter change
  (multi-value param). Higher blast radius.
- Risk: medium -- touches the appointments list filter contract; defer unless
  the owner explicitly wants combined filters.

## 8. Root-Cause Hypothesis

High confidence. The reduced click-through coverage is a direct, intentional
consequence of the NEW dashboard being a partial redesign delivered in wave
W2-6 (per the component header comment at
`tenant-dashboard.component.ts:6-15`): only the cards whose backend counts were
live at MVP (Pending/Approved/Rejected) were made clickable, and the remaining
cards were rendered as visible-but-inert placeholders pending the day-of-exam,
billing, and W3 change-request features. The click-through *infrastructure* is
sound and OLD-faithful (same status IDs, working query-param consumer); only
the per-card wiring is incomplete. The 4 role click-throughs are absent for a
separate reason (their cards were dropped -- G-09-01). So this is a coverage
gap created by phased delivery, not a broken mechanism.
