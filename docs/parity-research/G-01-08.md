## 1. ID & Summary
The NEW booking form adds a behavior the OLD app never had: when a booker turns
OFF the Applicant Attorney (AA) section, an ABP confirmation modal asks "Is the
Applicant Self-Represented?"; declining reverts the toggle back ON. NEW also
defaults the AA section enabled. OLD treated the AA section as simply optional --
no acknowledgement, no default-on gate. The disposition is "include" meaning KEEP
the NEW guard as-is: it exceeds OLD with no parity loss, so the "fix" here is
intentional non-action (confirm it stays, do not strip it).

## 4. Expected vs Actual
- OLD ("expected" under strict parity): AA section is optional and can be left empty / turned off with no prompt; default presentation does not force an acknowledgement. (`appointment.domain.ts` role-flag only; no confirmation.)
- NEW ("actual"): AA section defaults ON; turning it off triggers a mandatory "Is the Applicant Self-Represented?" modal whose decline reverts the toggle. Extra interaction step + different default vs OLD.
- Disposition resolves the tension in NEW's favor: the guard is a deliberate UX hardening (booker cannot silently omit the attorney without acknowledging self-representation). It is a superset of OLD behavior, so "logical parity" is preserved (the booker can still proceed without an AA) while adding a safety confirmation. No data, schema, or business-rule divergence.

## 8. Root-Cause Hypothesis
Not a defect -- a deliberate NEW-side UX addition. The "root cause" of the divergence is an intentional design choice made 2026-05-28 (cited in-code at `appointment-add.component.ts:481-487`) to prevent a booker from silently omitting the applicant attorney; OLD simply had no such safeguard. The disposition correctly treats it as a non-parity-breaking superset to retain. Confidence: high (OLD absence and NEW presence both directly verified; no server coupling).
