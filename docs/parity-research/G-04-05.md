## 1. ID & Summary
On booking submit, OLD sent ONE generic `Patient-Appointment-Pending.html`
email to the booker/patient (subject "...has been Pending."), CC'd the global
`clinicStaffEmail` ServerSetting, and -- only when the submitter was an
external user -- a second `Patient-Appointment-ApproveReject.html` to Staff
Supervisor + Clinic Staff. NEW replaces the single Pending template with a
per-recipient 3-way "Requested" fan-out (Office / Registered / Unregistered)
plus the same staff approve/reject leg. Same trigger, richer targeting,
"Pending" reworded to "Requested." Dispositioned `exclude` (keep NEW) per
Adrian directive 2026-05-08.

- Expected (OLD 1:1): one Pending email + CC clinicStaffEmail; conditional staff blast.
- Actual (NEW): three Requested templates partitioned by recipient class, no
  CC on the requested fan-out, same conditional staff approve/reject blast.
- Difference is intentional and approved, not a defect.

## 6. Why Excluded + Revisit-If
Excluded because the divergence is a deliberate, approved improvement: the
per-recipient fan-out preserves OLD intent ("we got your request" + staff
approve/reject notice) while better targeting recipients (registered party gets
a login CTA, unregistered party gets a tenant-prefilled register CTA). The CSV
note is explicit: "Do not revert to OLD single Pending template."

Revisit-if:
- An office relied on the dropped `clinicStaffEmail` CC on the patient-facing
  Pending email to track new requests. (Note: the office still gets its own
  dedicated `AppointmentRequestedOffice` email, so this risk is largely
  mitigated -- but a CC-only inbox that is NOT a Staff Supervisor / Clinic
  Staff user would no longer receive a copy on the requested fan-out.) The
  broader Approved-path CC gap is tracked separately as G-04-06.
- A directive reversal removes the "Requested" wording / 3-way split.
