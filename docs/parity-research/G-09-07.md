## 1. ID & Summary
OLD dashboard's "Approved Appointment" card displays a counter named
`UpcomingAppointment`/`upcomingAppointmentCount` but its click-through
navigates to the Approved status filter. The variable said "upcoming",
the filter said "approved" -- OLD conflated the two (no separate
future-date/"upcoming" filter ever existed). NEW exposes a clean
"Approved This Week" card backed by `approvedThisWeek`, click-through
goes to `appointmentStatus=2` (Approved). Decision: keep NEW's clean
Approved semantics; do not revert to OLD's mixed approved/upcoming.

## 4. Expected vs Actual (brief)
- Expected (parity goal): land on the Approved status list when the
  Approved card is clicked. Both OLD and NEW satisfy this.
- Actual deviation: OLD's underlying counter carried the vestigial
  "upcoming" name and conflated approved == upcoming; NEW drops the
  vague "upcoming" idea, renames to "Approved This Week", and scopes the
  count to a week window (the week-window aspect is tracked separately
  as G-09-05, included).

## 5. Repro Status
not-reproduced-static-confirmed. No defect to reproduce -- this is an

the OLD/NEW citations above.

## 6. Why Excluded + Revisit-If
Excluded because NEW's clean "Approved" semantics are strictly clearer
than OLD's "upcoming/approved" conflation, and the click-through
behavior is already equivalent (both land on Approved). The CSV note is
explicit: "Keep NEW clean status=Approved semantics; do not revert to
OLD mixed approved/upcoming."
Revisit if: a genuine future-date "upcoming appointments" view is later
requested as a distinct concept (would be a NEW feature, not a parity
revert), or if the separate week-window decision (G-09-05) changes the
counter's meaning in a way that affects this card's label.
