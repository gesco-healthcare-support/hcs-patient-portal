## Current state (verified 2026-04-17)

- `ExternalUserType.ClaimExaminer = 2` enum value exists.
- Role "Claim Examiner" is seeded and self-registrable via external signup.
- `GetExternalUserLookupAsync` filters CE out of results (users with the role don't appear in the external user lookup).
- `GetDisplayName` in `ExternalSignupAppService` at line 244 includes the label.
- No CE-specific permissions, controllers, UI routes, assignment link, or dashboard. The role has zero functional wiring.

## Official documentation

- [BLS Occupational Outlook -- Claims Adjusters/Examiners](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/business-and-financial/claims-adjusters-appraisers-examiners-and-investigators.htm) -- Defines examiner as post-adjuster reviewer who authorises payment, references medical evaluations, maintains claim files.
- [O*NET 13-1031.00 -- Claims Adjusters/Examiners](https://www.onetonline.org/link/summary/13-1031.00) -- Formal task list includes contacting doctors and coordinating medical evaluations.
- [DWC QME FAQ for injured workers](https://www.dir.ca.gov/dwc/medicalunit/faqiw.html) -- California does NOT define Claim Examiner as a regulated WC role; it's an insurance/TPA job title, not a DIR/WCAB-regulated function.
- [ABP Angular Permission Management](https://abp.io/docs/latest/framework/ui/angular/permission-management) -- `*abpPermission` directive + `PermissionService.getGrantedPolicy()` + navigation `requiredPolicy` are the three levers for conditional UI.
- [ABP Identity module](https://abp.io/docs/latest/modules/identity) -- Roles are runtime data; a seeded role with no permissions or routes functions as a label only.

## Community findings

- [Pacific Workers -- Complete Guide to QMEs](https://www.pacificworkers.com/blog/2025/july/complete-guide-to-qmes-in-ca-workers-comp-cases/) -- California CE typically works for carrier/TPA; authorises IME scheduling and approves medical-legal fees.
- [4 Corner Resources -- Claims Examiner job description](https://www.4cornerresources.com/job-descriptions/insurance-claims-examiner/) -- Reviews adjuster findings, authorises settlements, interfaces with medical specialists.
- [Indeed -- Claims Adjuster job description](https://www.indeed.com/hire/job-description/claims-adjuster) -- Examiner is the authorising/reviewing role, not the scheduling role, in most carriers.
- [Stack Overflow -- Show permissions based on features (ABP)](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/74312302/abp-io-show-permissions-based-on-features) -- Best practice: gate UI on permissions, not role names. A role with no permissions is dead weight.

## Recommended approach

Label each sub-question with a likely answer + confidence, then a keep-or-delete recommendation.

- **Sub-Q1: Is CE a future role stubbed out?** Likely YES (HIGH). Evidence: enum value exists, role is seeded, `GetDisplayName` covers it, but no permissions/controllers/UI. Classic half-wired feature.
- **Sub-Q2: Should CE see a subset of appointments (their assigned cases)?** Likely YES (MEDIUM). Industry norm: CE sees only appointments on claims they are assigned to -- read-only for scheduling data, read/write for payment authorisation. Requires a claim-to-CE assignment link that does not exist.
- **Sub-Q3: Why is CE excluded from `GetExternalUserLookupAsync`?** Likely defensive: self-service signup is intended for attorneys/patients; CE users would be invited by a tenant admin (carrier side) (MEDIUM).
- **Sub-Q4: Does CE need its own dashboard?** YES if the role is kept (MEDIUM). Dashboard would show "appointments on my assigned claims" + "pending fee authorisations." Without that, the role has no utility.
- **Decision: KEEP + FLAG AS DEFERRED** (HIGH). Don't delete the enum + label (data-model churn). DO:
  - Remove the seeded role from the self-service signup flow until wired.
  - Gate any CE-related UI on an ABP feature flag `Appointments.ClaimExaminerEnabled = false`.
  - When ready, add `ClaimExaminerAssignment` join entity (CE user -> claim/appointment), `Appointments.ClaimExaminer.View` / `.AuthorizePayment` permissions, guarded Angular route, dashboard widget filtered by assignment.

## Gotchas / blockers

- California "Claim Examiner" is not a regulated WCAB role; don't confuse with WCAB hearing representative or DWC auditor. The role here represents the insurance carrier / TPA job title.
- CE users can self-register today but hit a dead end (authenticate, see nothing). That is a UX defect today, not just a stub.
- Deleting the role is a breaking change for any tenant that already assigned a user to it; audit before removing.
- Cross-tenant question: are CEs tenant-scoped (per clinic) or cross-tenant (carrier-wide CE sees many clinics)? Major data-model consequences either way.

## Open questions

- Did the original devs intend CE to represent the insurance carrier adjuster/examiner, or the WCAB-adjacent hearing examiner? Product owner must pick one.
- Should CE assignments be at the claim level (all appointments on a claim) or per-appointment?
- Are CEs tenant-scoped or cross-tenant? Drives data model.
