## Industry / regulatory context

- [eCFR -- 45 CFR § 164.308 Administrative Safeguards](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-C/part-164/subpart-C/section-164.308) -- § 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) requires a written risk analysis; § 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B) requires risk management; § 164.308(a)(8) requires periodic evaluation.
- [eCFR -- 45 CFR Part 164 Subpart D (Breach Notification)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-C/part-164) -- §§ 164.400-414 set breach-notification obligations; 60-day ceiling for individual, media, and HHS.
- [HHS -- HIPAA & Cloud Computing](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/special-topics/health-information-technology/cloud-computing/index.html) -- Cloud providers handling ePHI are Business Associates; BAA is required; encryption does not exempt them.
- [HHS -- Sample BAA Provisions](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/covered-entities/sample-business-associate-agreement-provisions/index.html) -- HHS-published BAA template, directly usable as a starting point.
- [HHS -- Workers' Compensation Disclosures Guidance](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/guidance/disclosures-workers-compensation/index.html) -- Carriers/employers are NOT covered entities; IME providers who receive PHI from a covered-entity provider MAY be business associates of that provider.
- [HHS -- Breach Notification Rule](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/breach-notification/index.html) -- 60-day outer limit; annual roll-up allowed for sub-500-person breaches.
- [Cal. Civ. Code § 56.10 (CMIA)](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=56.10.&lawCode=CIV) -- California overlay: provider/contractor/health-plan disclosure rules. Penalties up to $25,000 per wilful violation.
- [Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.82](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=CIV&sectionNum=1798.82) -- California breach-notification statute; SB 446 (effective 2026-01-01) tightens timelines to 30 days for individuals and 15 days to AG for >500 residents.
- [HIPAA Journal -- HIPAA BAA Guide (2026 update)](https://www.hipaajournal.com/hipaa-business-associate-agreement/) -- Current practitioner guide on BAA scope and signatories.
- [Microsoft Learn -- Azure HIPAA/HITECH Compliance Offering](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-hipaa-hitech) -- Lists Azure services under the BAA.
- [AWS HIPAA Eligible Services Reference](https://aws.amazon.com/compliance/hipaa-eligible-services-reference/) -- Updated 2026-02-10; 166+ eligible services.
- [Cal. AG -- Data Breach Reporting Portal](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/databreach/reporting) -- Where >500-CA-resident breach notices must be filed.
- [Mehta & McConnell -- Does HIPAA Apply to Workers' Comp Claims?](https://mehtamcconnell.com/blog/does-hipaa-apply-to-workers-compensation-claims/) -- Practitioner breakdown of the IME-specific HIPAA question.
- [Troutman Pepper -- California SB 446 (2026-01-01)](https://www.troutmanprivacy.com/2025/10/california-amends-breach-notification-statute/) -- Summary of the amendment now in effect.

## Likely answer

**MEDIUM confidence**: No formal HIPAA compliance review has been performed. No BAAs signed (no cloud provider in play yet). No § 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) risk analysis documented. No HIPAA-specific counsel engaged. This is normal for a pre-production localhost codebase but becomes a blocker before first deploy -- especially because Gesco's business-associate status turns on who downstream receives the IME report.

**Assumptions that would flip this:**
- Discovery of an existing risk analysis document outside the repo (SharePoint, Google Drive, counsel's files).
- Existing BAA with a prior cloud vendor.
- Gesco is contractually a business associate to a covered-entity healthcare provider; if so, compliance artefacts should already exist somewhere.

## Recommended approach to obtain a definitive answer

- Ask Gesco business owner: "Has anyone ever performed a HIPAA risk analysis for Gesco or this product? Is it documented anywhere?"
- Ask: "Who are our downstream clients -- workers' comp carriers only, or any hospitals, health plans, or providers?" (Determines covered-entity-adjacent business-associate status.)
- Ask: "Do we have any existing BAAs on file -- Microsoft, AWS, Google, any software vendor?"
- Ask: "Have we ever engaged privacy/healthcare counsel (even a one-off consult)?"
- If all no: engage a California healthcare-privacy attorney for a scoped pre-deploy review (CMIA + HIPAA + SB 446).

## Default assumption if unanswered

Treat as: no HIPAA compliance work done. Pre-deploy blocker list: (1) written risk analysis, (2) BAA with chosen cloud host, (3) written breach-response plan, (4) CMIA + SB 446 breach-notification procedure, (5) one-time attorney review.

## Risk if assumption is wrong

Low downside to assuming nothing exists (redundant work at worst). High downside to assuming compliance is in place when it isn't: deploying ePHI without a BAA is an immediate violation (OCR fines up to $2.134M per year per violation category under the 2026 tier schedule), plus CMIA civil/administrative penalties up to $25,000 per wilful violation.
