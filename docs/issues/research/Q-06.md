# Q6: Minimum Advance Booking Window -- Research

**Severity**: Medium (refines BUG-09 fix scope)
**Status**: Open (verified 2026-04-17)
**Source files**:
- `src/HealthcareSupport.CaseEvaluation.Application/Appointments/AppointmentsAppService.cs` (`CreateAsync` -- no past-date or advance-window check)
- `angular/src/app/doctor-availabilities/...` (availability generator has no minimum-date UI)

---

## Current state (verified 2026-04-17)

- `AppointmentsAppService.CreateAsync` validates slot availability, location match, type match, date match, and time range -- but does not check whether the date is in the past or too close to today.
- BUG-09 already tracks the past-date acceptance; this research refines the fix.
- No "3 day", "advance", or "booking window" references anywhere in the codebase.
- Angular availability generator has no minimum-date constraint.

## Official documentation

- [8 CCR § 31.3 -- Scheduling Appointment with Panel QME](https://www.dir.ca.gov/t8/31_3.html) and [Cornell LII mirror](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/california/8-CCR-31.3) -- QME must offer an appointment within 90 days of request; waivable up to 120. No minimum-advance floor; only a maximum-delay ceiling.
- [8 CCR § 31.5 -- QME Replacement Requests](https://www.dir.ca.gov/t8/31_5.html) -- If 90/120-day ceiling missed, either party can demand replacement panel. No minimum-advance rule.
- [8 CCR § 34 -- Appointment Notification and Cancellation](https://www.dir.ca.gov/t8/34.html) -- Governs cancellation notice (6 business days), not advance-booking minimum.
- [8 CCR § 35 -- Exchange of Information](https://www.dir.ca.gov/t8/35.html) -- Records and advocacy letters must be served on opposing party **at least 20 days before** the evaluation. Closest thing to a regulatory floor: booking under 20 days out triggers the unrepresented-worker exception path.
- [8 CCR § 38 -- Medical Evaluation Time Frames](https://www.dir.ca.gov/t8/38.html) -- Governs report-turnaround timing, not booking lead time.
- [ABP Settings](https://docs.abp.io/en/abp/latest/Settings) -- Hierarchical scope User > Tenant > Global > Default; `TenantSettingManagementProvider` for persistence.

## Community findings

- [LFLM -- New Timeline to Set QME Evaluations](https://www.lflm.com/news-knowledge/new-timeline-to-set-qme-evaluations-amendment-to-regulations-for-medical-legal-evaluations/) -- Documents the 2023 amendment moving the ceiling from 60 to 90 days; confirms no minimum floor was added.
- [California Lawyers Association -- DWC Issues New QME Regs](https://calawyers.org/workers-compensation/dwc-issues-new-qme-regs/) -- Practitioner summary; silent on minimum-advance.
- [CAQME -- Legal deadlines for QME reports](https://caqme.com/what-are-the-legal-deadlines-for-qme-reports/) -- Practitioner deadline cheatsheet; confirms the 20-day records-exchange rule is the de-facto floor.

## Recommended approach

- **Likely window: 7-day minimum, configurable per tenant** (MEDIUM confidence). Regulation does not mandate a minimum-advance floor, but § 35's 20-day records-exchange rule makes anything shorter than 20 days a procedural risk. A 7-day operational floor (for scheduling mechanics, patient notification, interpreter booking) plus a soft warning under 20 days is defensible.
- **Fix BUG-09 first** (HIGH). Validate `AppointmentDate >= DateTime.UtcNow.Date` in `AppointmentManager.CreateAsync`. This is orthogonal to the advance-window policy.
- **Make the window a per-tenant ABP setting** (HIGH). Name: `CaseEvaluation.Appointments.MinimumAdvanceBookingDays`, default 7, scope User|Tenant|Global|Default. Rationale: tenants have different contractual SLAs; DWC could amend § 31.3 again.
- **Add a soft warning at 20 days** in the Angular booking form (MEDIUM): "Records exchange requires 20 days advance notice per 8 CCR § 35. Proceed?" -- warn, do not block.
- **Do NOT hardcode "3 days"** anywhere. No regulation or industry norm supports that specific number.

## Gotchas / blockers

- § 35 has an exception for unrepresented employees who self-schedule inside 20 days -- a hard block would violate the exception.
- Some tenants may need to bypass even a 7-day floor for urgent re-exams; admin override must be supported.
- Time-zone handling: clinics are in California; if API host runs UTC, same-day checks can be off by 8 hours.
- BUG-09 is the current gap; the minimum-advance window sits on top of that fix, not instead of it.

## Open questions

- Is the portal scheduling QMEs specifically (subject to § 31.3 ceiling) or also non-QME IMEs (more flexible)? Affects whether 20-day warning is relevant.
- Do tenants need different minimums? (If yes, setting vs const.) Almost certainly yes.
- Business days or calendar days? Records-exchange case law treats 20 as calendar days.
